# Shared Context -- career-ops

## North Star

This fork is optimized for a Drupal professional who wants to win a better mix of:

- strong full-time roles
- long-term contracts
- high-quality freelance projects
- architecture and rescue work

## Archetypes

| Archetype | What they buy |
|-----------|---------------|
| Senior Drupal Developer | Delivery speed and reliable execution |
| Drupal Consultant | Advisory judgment and client communication |
| Drupal Technical Lead | Architecture, coordination, and standards |
| Headless Drupal Engineer | Modern frontend and API-led delivery |
| Drupal Solutions Architect | System design, integrations, governance |
| Migration Specialist | Legacy rescue and content/platform transitions |

## Evaluation Lens

Score every opportunity using both technical and commercial fit:

1. Drupal relevance
2. Seniority and ownership fit
3. Scope clarity
4. Budget or comp fit
5. Remote and timezone fit
6. Client or company quality
7. Delivery risk
8. Strategic value for the portfolio

## Framing By Opportunity Type

- Full-time role: emphasize stability, leadership, maintainability, and platform ownership.
- Contract role: emphasize speed, clarity, and immediate impact.
- Freelance lead: emphasize problem diagnosis, delivery confidence, and proposal discipline.
- Rescue project: emphasize migrations, debugging, performance, and governance.

## Cross-cutting Advantages

Frame the candidate as a builder who can:

- untangle messy Drupal estates
- deliver without fluff
- communicate well with both stakeholders and developers
- move between implementation and architecture

## Rate And Comp Guidance

- Full-time: compare against `target_salary_usd` from `config/profile.yml`. For Gerardo's current profile, treat `$4,200-$5,000 monthly` as the target band and `$4,200 monthly` as the minimum acceptable floor.
- Contract or freelance: compare against the freelance floor from `config/profile.yml`, not the salary-equivalent floor. For Gerardo's current profile, treat `$42-$50/h` as the realistic target band and `$40/h` as the minimum acceptable floor.
- If a contract is priced by day, compare against `freelance_realistic_day_rate_usd` in `config/profile.yml`.
- If the opportunity is vague and low-budget, score it down hard.

### Compensation Scoring Rule

- Full-time below `$4,200 monthly` -> strong negative signal unless there is a clear upside in role quality or future growth.
- Full-time inside `$4,200-$5,000 monthly` -> acceptable to good.
- Full-time above `$5,000 monthly` -> strong positive signal.
- Contract or freelance below `$40/h` -> strong negative signal by default.
- Contract or freelance in `$42-$50/h` or equivalent day-rate -> target fit.
- Contract or freelance above target band -> strong positive signal if scope and client quality are solid.

## Output Choice

- Full-time role -> tailored CV plus application notes
- Contract role -> tailored CV plus delivery framing
- Freelance lead -> capability statement, proposal outline, or qualification questions

## Rules

### Never

1. Invent Drupal experience, metrics, or client logos
2. Promise delivery dates the user did not approve
3. Treat low-budget unclear work as high-quality pipeline
4. Submit without review

### Always

1. Read `cv.md`, `article-digest.md`, and `config/profile.yml`
2. Decide whether the opportunity is full-time, contract, or freelance
3. Map requirements to real proof points
4. Flag bad-fit work early
5. Register the opportunity in the tracker
